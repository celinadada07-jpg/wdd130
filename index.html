Rivers State, Nigeria

Rivers State is a state in the southern region of Nigeria, located in the Niger Delta. It is bordered by Bayelsa State to the west, Delta State to the northwest, Imo State and Abia State to the north, Akwa Ibom State to the east, and the Atlantic Ocean to the south. The state is a major center for Nigeria’s oil and gas industry, commerce, and maritime activities, and is known for its rivers, mangrove forests, and rich cultural heritage. Port Harcourt, the state capital and largest city, is an important industrial and economic hub often referred to as the “Garden City.”
